How the British Viewed the Opium Trade

By Arshan Hejazi, for class HI367

Welcome

This online exhibition explores how British officials, in the late 19th century, rationalized and defended the opium trade. We draw primarily from the Records of the Royal Commission on Opium: Minutes of Evidence (1894).

Despite moral objections about opium’s impact on China, British testimony consistently emphasized the trade’s necessity for colonial revenues in India. Navigate through the pages to read key quotes from the commission’s report, a thorough analysis, and a visual illustration showcasing the stark human cost of these policies.
